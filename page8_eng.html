Sport
Sports achievements of Kazakhstanis occupy a special place. In 1994, at the Winter Olympic Games in Lillehammer, the first triumph of independent Kazakhstan in big sports took place. This was the beginning of our sports biography. Then skier Vladimir SMIRNOVhe won the first gold for our country. In the future, Kazakhstani athletes will bring 63 more Olympic medals to the piggy bank. For 30 years, boxing has undoubtedly become the hallmark of Kazakhstan's sporting achievements. In 1996, at the XXVI Summer Olympic Games in Atlanta (Georgia, USA), the Balkhash boxer Vasily ZHIROV confidently declared himself. In addition to the title of Olympic champion, he became the owner of the most honorable prize - the Val Barker Cup, which was owned by fighters from only eight countries in the history of the Olympics. Ermakhan IBRAIMOV, Bekzat Sattarkhanov, Bakhtiar ARTAYEV, Bakyt SARSEKBAYEV, Serik SAPIYEV and Daniyar YELEUSINOV became the same unsurpassed gold-bearing boxers in their sport. A special line should be marked with the name of Gennady GOLOVKIN.His name is heard not only among our compatriots. Gennady has 41 victories on his account. In 2016, Golovkin entered the Guinness Book of Records. He became the world champion with the highest percentage of wins by knockout – 91.67%. GGG - the abbreviation has become a world-famous brand. Gennady Golovkin enters the ring in the national Kazakh chapan, and it has become a tradition. Also, over 30 years of independence, athletes, wrestlers, swimmers, weightlifters, cyclists, figure skaters and biathletes have brought success to Kazakhstan's sport. The weightlifting school has nurtured a large number of champion weightlifters over the years of independence. In 1996 Anatoly KHRAPATYhe brought Kazakhstan the first gold at the World Championship. Later, Ilya ILYIN, Nijat Rahimov, Svetlana PODOBEDOVA and others will continue this legendary list. In cycling, cyclist Alexander VINOKUROV replenished the piggy bank of gold medals, who won first place at the 2012 Olympic Games in London. The champion of the Olympic Games in London (2012), the silver medalist of the Olympic Games in Beijing (2008) and the bronze medalist of the Olympic Games in Rio de Janeiro (2016) in the triple jump, the 2010 World indoor champion in the triple jump are the titles of our athlete Olga RYPAKOVA. Alexey POLTORANIN - Kazakhstani skier, two-time bronze medalist of the 2013 World Championships, Honored Master of Sports of the Republic of Kazakhstan. At the next Summer Olympics, Dmitry BALANDIN opened the winning path in swimming competitions. Kazakhstan's record holder at distances of 50, 100 and 200 meters breaststroke, Honored Master of Sports of the Republic of Kazakhstan (2016). The first ever representative of Kazakhstan to win Olympic gold in swimming. In figure skating, Kazakhstan was on the Olympic podium for the first time in 2014, when Denis TENhe won a bronze medal. The silver medalist of the 2019 World Championship, the silver medalist of the 2019 Four Continents Championship, the silver medalist of the 2019 Winter Universiade is our talented figure skater Elizabeth TURSYNBAYEVA.
