Closure Semipalatinsk landfill
A few months before independence, on August 29, 1991, the Semipalatinsk nuclear test site was closed by Decree of Nursultan NAZARBAYEV. After the collapse of the USSR, a huge stock of nuclear weapons remained in Kazakhstan. For 52 years of existence, 500 explosions were carried out at the Semipalatinsk nuclear test site. On the initiative of the leadership of our country, August 29 was declared the Day of International Action against Nuclear Tests. This bold step at that time contributed to the strengthening of trust on the part of major world powers and ensured respect for our republic.
Qusihad, 2022
